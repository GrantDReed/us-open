# 2026 U.S. Open Fantasy Golf Tier Sheet — Shinnecock Hills (June 18–21, 2026)

**Bottom line: Scottie Scheffler is the clear, standalone £4M anchor at a consensus ~+520, and the only defensible same-tier overrides this week are a risk-based hold of Brooks Koepka at £2M (he's playing through a hand injury) and hot-form promotions of major champion Aaron Rai and RBC Canadian Open winner Bud Cauley within the £2M tier — everything else defers to the odds-then-form methodology.** The field is confirmed at exactly 156 (USGA announced the completed field June 14, 2026), so tier sizes hold at 6 / 15 / 45 / 90. That cut profile drives the cut-risk reads below.

## Recommendations
- **Prioritize cut security.** With under 40% of the field reaching the weekend and worst-round-of-day penalties for missed cuts, lean on reliable cut-makers over top-heavy win-equity picks.
- **£2M cut-security targets:** Hideki Matsuyama (12/13 U.S. Open cuts), Harris English (10/10), J.J. Spaun, Aaron Rai. **£1M cut-makers over dart throws:** Sam Stevens (3/3), Michael Kim, Chris Kirk.
- **Be wary of high-variance bombers** (Åberg, DeChambeau) and chronic U.S. Open cut-missers (Conners); the fescue/precision premium punishes them.
- **Situational reads that change the plan:** (a) If any pick posts a pre-tournament WD, react early — at the cut, free transfers can only move to the same or a lower tier, so favor picks you won't be stranded by. (b) If the weather turns benign and firm/fast chaos is reduced, the cut becomes less random and form/odds favorites get safer. (c) If wind ramps up, over-weight steady ball-strikers (Fitzpatrick, Rai, Henley, Rose) over putter-dependent picks.
